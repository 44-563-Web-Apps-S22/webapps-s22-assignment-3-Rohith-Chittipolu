Chicago Cheetah

The mascot of Chicago Soccer team is "Chicago Cheetah."

Chicago Cheetah, has been the mascot of the Chicago Soccer team for more than 50 years, first at Chicago Stadium (1969–1994), and now at the United Center (1994–present). He has become just as popular as some of the franchise's most notable figures, He is one of the longest-tenured mascots in the Game and in all of professional sports. The Teams's first Public Relations Manager and Stadium Announcer. Cheetah was one of the promotional tools general manager Rohith employed to boost the Teams' arena entertainment and attendance.

  • Chicago Cheetah unlike real cheetah is red in color with black spots.
  • Among Cheetah's many talents are tumbling, gymnastics, break dancing, reading, officiating the games.
  • Unlike real cheetah Chicago Cheetah does not have a tail.
